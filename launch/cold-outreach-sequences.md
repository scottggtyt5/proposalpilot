## SENDING BEST PRACTICES

### Timing & Frequency

**Optimal Send Times**:
- **Agencies**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9 AM - 11 AM (local recipient time)
- **Freelancers**: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM - 2 PM (more flexible schedules)
- **LinkedIn DMs**: Weekdays, 9 AM - 5 PM (during work hours)

**Frequency**:
- **Agency Sequence**: Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 21 (21-day cycle)
- **Freelancer Sequence**: Day 0, 3, 7, 14 (14-day cycle, shorter consideration window)
- **LinkedIn Sequence**: Connection request → +2 days → +5 days (avoid spam-like cadence)

**Avoid**:
- Weekends and holidays
- Monday mornings (inbox overload)
- Friday afternoons (low engagement)
- More than 1 email per sequence per day

### Deliverability & Domain Setup

- **From Address**: Use a personal email from a real team member, not noreply@
- **From Name**: "{{first_name}}" or "FirstName LastName" (not company name for cold outreach)
- **SPF/DKIM/DMARC**: Set up before sending (reduces spam folder)
- **List Hygiene**: Remove hard bounces and complainers before next send
- **Unsubscribe Link**: Legal requirement; include in footer (improves trust)

### A/B Testing Protocol

- Test ONE element per send (subject line OR body, not both)
- Split: 50/50 for smaller lists, 80/20 for larger lists (80% winner variant, 20% challenger)
- Measure: Open rate (subject line), Click-through rate (CTA copy), Reply rate (value prop)
- Confidence threshold: 100+ opens minimum before declaring winner
- Repeat: Run 2-3 tests per sequence for continuous improvement

### Email-Level Performance

**Email 1 (Cold Open)**: Should have highest open rate (25-40%)
- If below 20%: Test subject lines
- Red flag: Open rate drops significantly, personalization may be off

**Email 2 (Follow-up)**: Should have 15-25% open rate
- If below 10%: Sequence may be too aggressive; lengthen gap
- Red flag: Reply rate high but positive replies low = messaging miss

**Email 3 (Social Proof)**: Should have 12-20% open rate
- If clicks drop: Social proof not resonating; adjust case study
- Red flag: Low reply rate = audience may not be right fit

**Email 4 (Value-Add)**: Should have 10-15% open rate
- If below 8%: Content not valuable enough; test different topic
- Red flag: Click rate low = CTA not aligned to content

**Email 5 (Breakup)**: Should have 8-12% open rate + highest positive reply rate
- If open rate high but replies low: Reverse psychology working (good sign)
- Red flag: Unsubscribes spike = list quality issue

## ADVANCED TACTICS

### Warming Your List

**Cold outreach converts poorly without warming**. Before sending Sequence 1:

1. **LinkedIn Connection** (optional, 5-7 days before): Send connection request with note referencing their work
2. **LinkedIn Engagement** (optional, 3-5 days before): Like/comment on 2-3 recent posts
3. **Email Introduction** (if possible): Have a mutual contact send a warm intro

**Expected lift from warming**:
- Cold outreach: 0.5-1% conversion
- Warmed list: 1.5-3% conversion

### Segmentation by Buying Signal

**High-Intent Signals** (prioritize these):
- Recently posted about proposals, client work, or business growth
- Changed jobs in the last 3 months (new priorities)
- Company is hiring (growth = more client work = more proposals)
- Engaged with competitor content
- Mentioned pain point in post/article

**Low-Intent Signals** (deprioritize or skip):
- No recent activity
- Rarely engages with business content
- Works at Fortune 500 (unlikely to use freelancer tool)
- Explicit "not looking for sales tools" statement

### Reverse Breakup Email

If someone goes dark but doesn't unsubscribe after Email 5:

**Day 35 - Curiosity Email**:
"Hi {{first_name}}, quick question: Is there something specific holding you back, or did we just miss your radar? Happy to adjust the pitch. Otherwise, no worries."

- Gives them an "out" (permission to be honest)
- Shows genuine interest in feedback
- May uncover hidden objections worth addressing

## NOTES ON TONE & AUTHENTICITY

**What Works**:
- Short sentences (easier to scan)
- Conversational language (avoid jargon)
- One clear ask per email (don't overwhelm)
- Data when available (specific beats generic)
- Genuine curiosity (ask questions, want feedback)

**What Doesn't**:
- ALL CAPS and excessive punctuation!!!
- Generic statements ("we're the best", "world-class")
- Trying too hard to be funny (land-misses undermine credibility)
- Fake familiarity ("just following up on our chat" when there was none)
- Too many CTAs (one clear ask per email)

**The ProposalPilot Voice**:
Fast, straightforward, respectful of time. No fluff. Every word earns its place.
